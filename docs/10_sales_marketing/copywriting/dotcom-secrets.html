2. The Attractive Character

How to build a personality-driven brand people want to follow

Core Concept: People don't buy from companies - they buy from people. Every successful brand has an "Attractive Character" (the face of the brand) that tells stories, shares vulnerabilities, and builds a tribe.

The 4 Elements of an Attractive Character:

  • Backstory: Your origin story (how you got here, struggles overcome)
  • Parables: Teaching through stories (not preachy, relatable)
  • Character Flaws: Vulnerability (shows you're human, builds trust)
  • Polarity: Standing for something (having enemies unites your tribe)

Russell's Application: Russell himself is the Attractive Character for ClickFunnels:

  • Backstory: College student selling potato gun DVDs â†’ Built ClickFunnels after years of frustration
  • Parables: Every book/podcast shares stories of his failures and wins
  • Flaws: Openly discusses failed products, bad decisions, struggles with perfectionism
  • Polarity: "Funnel Hackers" (us) vs. "traditional marketers" (them)

3. The Soap Opera Sequence

The email sequence that converts cold leads into hot buyers

Core Concept: When someone joins your list (opts in for lead magnet), send them a strategic 5-email sequence that builds a relationship BEFORE pitching. Just like soap operas hook viewers with cliffhangers, your emails hook readers with open loops.

The 5-Email Sequence:

  • Email 1 - Set the Stage: Deliver lead magnet + introduce yourself as the guide
  • Email 2 - Open Loop #1: Share backstory (part 1) + hint at transformation
  • Email 3 - Open Loop #2: Continue backstory (epiphany moment) + more intrigue
  • Email 4 - Close Loop: Finish story + reveal solution (your product/service)
  • Email 5 - Soft Pitch: Make the offer (but don't hard sell - offer value)

Why It Works: You build trust FIRST (Attractive Character) before asking for the sale. By Email 5, they feel like they know you and want your solution.

7. Traffic (Who, Where, Bait, Result)

The simple formula for getting traffic to your funnels

Core Concept: Traffic isn't random. You need to answer 4 questions before running ads or creating content:

  • WHO is your dream customer? (Demographics + psychographics)
  • WHERE are they congregating? (What websites, groups, podcasts, influencers?)
  • What BAIT will attract them? (What lead magnet solves their #1 pain?)
  • What RESULT do you want? (Email opt-in? Purchase? Call booked?)

Russell's Application: ClickFunnels targets "entrepreneurs who want to grow online" â†’ They congregate on marketing podcasts, Facebook groups, YouTube â†’ Russell's bait is free books on funnels â†’ Result is ClickFunnels trial signup

Note: This framework is expanded massively in Traffic Secrets (98,000 words dedicated to traffic).
