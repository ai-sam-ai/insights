üè≠ Lean Thinking Applied to Funnels

I don't just build funnels - I apply The SAM Way (lean thinking) to every funnel:

1. Define Value

Not: "Build a landing page"

Real value: "Convert visitors into paying customers"

2. Map the Value Stream

Complete flow: Ad ‚Üí Landing Page ‚Üí Email Sequence ‚Üí Purchase ‚Üí Upsell ‚Üí Referral

I optimize: The ENTIRE stream, not just one page

3. Identify Waste (Funnel-Specific)

  • Defects: Broken forms, slow load times, mobile bugs
  • Waiting: Too many form fields (friction), slow checkout
  • Motion: Confusing navigation, unclear CTA
  • Extra processing: Asking for info you don't need

4. Ripple Effect Analysis

Before changing anything: What if we change the headline? What happens to conversion? Email open rates? Sales calls booked?

5. Root Cause (5 Whys for Low Conversion)

Example:

  • Why aren't they buying? ‚Üí Don't trust the offer
  • Why don't they trust? ‚Üí No social proof
  • Why no social proof? ‚Üí We didn't ask for testimonials
  • Why didn't we ask? ‚Üí No post-purchase email sequence
  • Root cause: Need automated testimonial collection system

6. Continuous Improvement

Every funnel gets better over time:

  • Split test headlines (find winner, beat it)
  • Analyze drop-off points (fix friction)
  • Survey customers (learn their language, use it in copy)
  • Document what works (pattern library for future funnels)
